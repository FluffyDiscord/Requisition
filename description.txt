Adds a networked bulk storage system to Terraria. Store thousands of item stacks across tiered Storage Disks, access and craft from them through a Terminal, and take your collection with you between worlds.

[b]This mod is in alpha — bugs are expected. Please report anything you find![/b]
[hr][/hr]

[h2]Storage Disks[/h2]
Six tiers of Storage Disks, each upgradable from the previous tier in the Terminal (Disks Tab).  Only the Basic Disk can be crafted.
Upgrading a disk preserves its identity and all stored items — nothing is lost when upgrade.  Items are not stored on the disk themselves. Each disk has a GUID which matches to a table that stores the items.  Even if you lose the disk, your items are safe.

[h2]Drive Bay[/h2]
A placeable block that holds up to 40 Storage Disks. Multiple Drive Bays can be linked into a single network. The Drive Bay is simply the physical housing for disks.

[h2]Terminal[/h2]
The access point for the storage network. Open it to:
[list]
[*] Browse all items across every connected Drive Bay in a scrollable, searchable grid
[*] Deposit items from your inventory into the network (shift-click or drag)
[*] Withdraw items directly to your inventory
[*] Craft using items stored in the network, with full multi-step recipe support
[/list]
The Terminal window is draggable and resizable.

[b]Filtering & Sorting[/b]
16 category filter buttons (left-click to isolate, right-click to toggle)
7 sort modes with ascending/descending toggle (right-click the active button to flip direction)

Favorited items always sort to the top regardless of other sort settings. Favorites persist per character.

[b]Search Prefixes[/b]
[list]
[*] Type normally to search by item name
[*] Start with [b]#[/b] to search by tooltip and item properties (e.g. [b]#bait[/b], [b]#damage[/b], [b]#accessory[/b])
[*] Start with [b]@[/b] to search by mod name (e.g. [b]@calamity[/b], [b]@terraria[/b])
[/list]

[b]Storage Count Tooltips[/b]
After opening a Terminal, hovering any item anywhere in the game shows how many of that item are in your storage.

[b]Crafting Tab[/b]
[list]
[*] Multi-step crafting: if you lack an intermediate ingredient, the Terminal will craft it automatically
[*] Toggle to show uncraftable recipes
[*] Toggle recursive crafting — highlights recipes whose ingredients can themselves be crafted. Right-click drag the checkbox to set recursion depth
[*] Right-click an ingredient in the detail view to navigate to its recipe, with full back-navigation history
[*] Cycle between recipe variants for items with multiple crafting paths
[/list]

[h2]Crafting Core[/h2]
Place one on your network to enable Terminal crafting. Load it with crafting station items —  including those from other mods — to make those stations available for crafting. Demon Altars and Crimson Altars are provided to enable recipes that require them.

[h2]Favorited Recipes Panel[/h2]
A separate floating panel that tracks your favorited recipes. For each recipe it shows the output item and every ingredient with a live have/need count drawn from both storage and your inventory. Collapsible, pinnable, and is transparent when pinned with the inventory closed.

[h2]Crafting Tree[/h2]
A visual graph explorer for item relationships. Hover any item in your inventory and press a configurable hotkey to open.
[list]
[*] [b]Bidirectional[/b] — the right side shows what an item crafts into; the left side shows the ingredients needed to create it. Right-click nodes to expand or collapse branches.
[*] [b]Info Panel[/b] — left-click a node to select it and reveal a sidebar with all non-crafting sources: NPC drops (with drop chance and stack range), NPC shop availability, and Shimmer transmutations.
[*] [b]Terminal integration[/b] — middle-click any node while a Terminal is open to jump directly to that recipe in the crafting tab.
[*] [b]Minimap[/b] — a corner overview of the full graph. Click and drag to navigate.
[*] Animated expand/collapse transitions, category-colored nodes. Draggable, resizable, minimizable window.
[/list]

[h2]Disk Archiving[/h2]
Middle-click a disk in your inventory to archive it. An archived disk embeds all its stored items into itself, removes them from the world's storage, and can be carried to a different world and unarchived there. Middle-click again to unarchive — inserting it into any Drive Bay restores all items.

[h2]Disk Backups[/h2]
TerraStorage automatically backs up your storage as you play. Up to 3 rolling backups are kept per world — the current session is updated continuously, and older sessions are preserved as you come and go.

Restore from the [b]Client Config[/b] page: pick a world, pick a slot, click Restore. Load the world and the backup replaces your current storage state.

Dedicated server admins can use the [b]tsrestore[/b] console command:
[list]
[*] [b]tsrestore list[/b] — show available backups with timestamps
[*] [b]tsrestore [0|1|2][/b] — apply a restore immediately without restarting the world
[/list]

[h2]Disk Recovery[/h2]
Opened from the Drive Bay UI. Shows all known disk records in world storage, including orphaned disks whose physical item was lost. Select a lost disk, place a blank replacement disk of matching tier in the slot, and click Restore to recover full access to its contents.

[h2]Multiplayer[/h2]
Fully multiplayer-compatible. Storage operations are server-authoritative; clients send requests and receive synced state back. An optional [b]Predictive Sync Mode[/b] (server config) uses lightweight delta packets instead of full disk broadcasts, reducing network traffic.

[h2]Mod Compatibility[/h2]
[list]
[*] Accepts any placeable item as a crafting station — no hardcoded station list
[*] Damage class filters catch modded subclasses (Calamity Rogue, True Melee, etc.)
[*] Disk data is preserved through all storage, cloning, and network operations
[*] Does not scan the world for tiles; all station availability is driven by items in the Crafting Core
[/list]
